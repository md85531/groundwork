Your 2× / week strength session

Two easy rounds, ~20 min. Light and controlled — stop on any pinch.

  • Goblet squat — 10
  • One-arm dumbbell row — 10 each side
  • Dumbbell floor press (on your mat) — 10
  • Dumbbell glute bridge — 12
  • Bird-dog — 8 each side
  • Dead bug — 8 each side
At the gym, machines give you more back support anytime: leg press, seated row, lat pulldown, leg curl. Same workout, steadier.

Back-safe until you're cleared

Skip heavy deadlifts, back squats, overhead pressing, and any loaded twisting. The glute bridges, bird-dogs, and dead bugs are the ones quietly protecting your lower back.

No-cook grab list

Protein anchors

  • Greek yogurt · cottage cheese
  • Pre-boiled eggs · egg bites
  • Rotisserie / pre-cooked grilled chicken
  • Deli turkey roll-ups · string cheese
  • Tuna or salmon packets
  • Protein shake (ready-to-drink) · jerky

Fiber boosters

  • Raspberries (the fiber champ) · apple, pear with skin
  • Avocado · chia or ground flax in yogurt
  • Steam-in-bag broccoli, green beans
  • Baby carrots · snap peas · cucumber + hummus
  • TJ's steamed lentil / black bean pouches
  • Wasa or whole-grain crackers · bagged popcorn

Combo grabs — the anti-fast-food move

  • Greek yogurt + raspberries + chia
  • Cottage cheese + steamed edamame
  • Turkey roll-ups + baby carrots + hummus
  • Tuna packet + whole-grain crackers + cucumber
  • Bean & cheese on a high-fiber tortilla
Fiber goal: work toward 25–30g a day, but add it gradually and drink water as you go — a sudden jump can backfire.
The two rules

The floor is one thing. A rough day means a 5-minute walk or one round or a single anchor — and that fully counts.

No fixed days. "Two strength sessions this week, whenever" survives a tired or busy week far better than locking in set days.
